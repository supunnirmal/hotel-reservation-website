Visited the Srilanka....

I thought Sri Lanka to be one of the most beautiful countries I have ever been to. Wonderful climate, scenery and endless sites of interests. You could easily spen many many trips exploring the different towns and regions. Just be prepared. It's not the easiest country to travel around, especially now because of the ethnic tensions. I've travelled a lot in Asia, but found dealing with people in the tourist industry here immensely frustrating. We met many wonderful people in Sri Lanka, but with a few exceptions they were unconnected with the tourist trade. I have read some comments from other posters expressing this and having been there now I would have to concur. You get ripped off and/ or hassled at every opportunity. The best thing to do is get into the country and run far far away from the tourist track. Try and go local as much as you can. Scams abound everywhere, and the "tourist" price, or whatever price quoted will always be about ten times what you should pay. Basically the industry is immature, in the "gouge the foreigners" mode, especially given the shrinkage caused by the simmering war. You can't blame the locals when even the government endorses this practice. I got the best experience by selecting the sutiable hotel that provide best facilities in my budget. The hotel I stayed inprovided endless opportunuties to experience the true SriLankan culture.

The experience we get from Lotus Hotel is a timeless classic: a versatile, gives the power to heal, rejuvenate, inspire, and stimulate the senses. What the traditional home did in our history books, this hotel strives to do today.
